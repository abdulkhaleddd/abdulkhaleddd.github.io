date: 2026-03-17

A clue that's technically correct but unpleasant to solve is a failure. 
The test I apply to every clue before it goes in a grid: if a solver 
gets the answer and reads the clue back, do they feel satisfied? 
Or do they feel cheated?

The wordplay should confirm the answer, not ambush the solver with 
something they couldn't have reasonably parsed. That's the line I try to walk.
